🔬 NMIBC Care Pathway ▼

Principles of TURBT

  • Obtain preoperative CTU prior to OR to determine need for ureteroscopy
  • Bilateral retrograde pyelograms for patients who cannot have CTU
  • No need for office cysto if tumor is obvious on imaging
  • Preop U/A with reflex urine culture
  • If disease is possibly non-invasive, order peri-operative intravesical Gemcitabine with case request
  • Document exam under anesthesia (EUA dot phrase) with each initial resection or re-resection with concern for disease progression
  • Re-resect if: known residual disease and bladder preservation desired; any high-grade disease without muscularis propria on pathology; any T1 tumors, even if muscularis propria is in the specimen
  • Peri-operative Gemcitabine preferably administered in OR for tumors thought to be non-invasive
Avoid peri-operative Gemcitabine if bladder perforation is suspected.
  • For recurrent non-invasive disease, consider enhanced cystoscopy TURBT (i.e. Blue-Light). Order Cysview with case request.
  • Clinical SUO Fellows are the preferred surgeons for TURBT requests that come from outside of the UroOnc Division
  • Use a shared TURBT case model whenever possible to expedite surgical care and maximize OR utilization

Principles of Intravesical Therapy

  • Complete resection prior to initiating intravesical therapy
  • Treatments should begin 2–4 weeks after resection
  • High-risk (HG Ta >3cm, HG T1, CIS): BCG 1st line; Gem/Doce 2nd line with 2yr maintenance; re-induction BCG 3rd line; Adstiladrin or Inlexzo for BCG-unresponsive CIS (see below); consider clinical trials
  • Intermediate-risk (HG Ta <3cm): BCG, Gem/Doce, or trial of surveillance alone
  • BCG dose reduction as needed per supply or tolerance; down to 10% (5mg TICE) can still be effective
  • Maintenance BCG at 3, 6, and 12 months from first induction dose. At least 2 years of maintenance is ideal; can extend to 3 years at surgeon discretion if supply allows.
  • Recurrent low-grade Ta: Induction + maintenance Gemcitabine monthly x 24mo (1st line); Mitomycin C (2nd line); BCG or Gem/Doce also options
BCG Shortage: Prioritize induction for HG T1 and CIS, plus continuation of initiated courses. 2 years of maintenance may not be possible with current shortage. Gem/Doce as alternative. BCG Request Form for New Starts →

BCG-Unresponsive CIS: Adstiladrin & Inlexzo

Important: Both agents are extremely expensive. Patients must meet FDA-approved criteria, which require the presence of CIS (with or without papillary tumors) and BCG-unresponsive disease. These are options for patients who refuse or are unfit for cystectomy. Both require approval from the Division Chief and can only be used at Shadyside at this time.

Adstiladrin (nadofaragene firadenovec-vncg)

  • Non-replicating adenoviral vector gene therapy delivering interferon-α2b to the urothelium
  • Schedule: 75 mL intravesical instillation every 3 months (quarterly)
  • Continue as long as no evidence of high-grade recurrence or unacceptable toxicity
  • FDA approved Dec 2022 for BCG-unresponsive NMIBC with CIS ± papillary tumors

Inlexzo (gemcitabine intravesical system / TAR-200)

  • Intravesical drug-releasing system (iDRS) providing sustained local gemcitabine delivery over 3 weeks per cycle
  • Induction: Inserted every 3 weeks for 6 months (up to 8 doses)
  • Maintenance: Every 12 weeks for up to 18 months (up to 6 doses)
  • Total treatment duration up to ~2 years (up to 14 cycles)
  • Placed in-office via urinary catheter without general anesthesia; removed after 3-week indwelling period
  • FDA approved Sep 2025 for BCG-unresponsive NMIBC with CIS ± papillary tumors

Surveillance for NMIBC

  • U/A on arrival to clinic
  • Antibiotic prophylaxis not necessary; consider if reusable cystoscope or patient has history of UTI with surveillance cysto
  • Fellows follow patients they resect; patients continue with subsequent fellows
  • Patient's bladder cancer pathology history must be in every surveillance cysto note
  • Date, modality, and result of most recent upper tract imaging must be in every surveillance cysto note
  • NMIBC low/intermediate/high-risk surveillance dot phrase must be in every surveillance cysto note
  • Once surveillance complete, send letter to patient and PCP with triggers for re-referral (gross hematuria, micro hematuria, or masses on imaging)
📋 Cystectomy: Preoperative Evaluation ▼

Before the Clinic Visit

  • Obtain notes from outside urologist, all bladder cancer pathology reports, and archive cross-sectional abdominal/pelvic imaging (CT or MRI) if within 6 months
  • Have most-recent TURBT pathology reviewed if not previously reviewed at Michigan Medicine

During the Clinic Visit

  • If ≥ cT2 or N+, assure neoadjuvant chemo considered; arrange hem/onc referral if appropriate
  • Discuss early ambulation, incentive spirometer, expect 3–5 day stay
  • Increase activity (walking, cardio, MSHOP) beginning 4 weeks pre-op; provide MSHOP bag
  • Stop smoking ≥4 weeks prior; refer to cessation if needed

Case Request & Pre-Op Orders

  • Pre-op H&P/consent; anesthesia eval and medical clearances PRN
  • Labs: Comp, CBCP, PT, PTT, INR, T&S, U/A with reflex culture; EKG within 1 year
  • ET Consult — mark preferred stoma site; add alternate if surgeon preference
  • Nutrition Consult
  • Staging: CT Urogram or A/P and Chest CT within 6 weeks of surgery
  • Cefoxitin 30 mg/kg (or alt if allergic) IV on call to OR
  • Acetaminophen 1000mg PO 30 min prior to OR
  • Alvimopan 12mg PO 30min–5hrs prior, then 12mg BID x 7 days
Alvimopan: Avoid if opioids >7 consecutive days prior, liver failure, kidney failure, or GI obstruction. D/C on POD 2 if not requiring opioids.

Patient Instructions (Surgery Schedulers)

  • Bring gum or hard candy for post-op
  • NPO: No solid food 8hrs prior (NPO after midnight for first-start); clear fluids up to 2hrs prior; no bowel prep
  • Carb loading: 36 oz white grape juice (100% pure) — 24 oz 7–10pm night prior; 12 oz day of surgery up to 2hrs prior
🩺 Cystectomy: Intraoperative ▼

Intraoperative Protocol

  • SCDs placed prior to induction
  • 5000 units SQ heparin post induction
  • OG tube — remove at extubation
  • Core temp >36°C
  • IVF: 3 cc/kg/hr; SPV and PPV directed; vasopressor and 250cc boluses for hemodynamic goals
  • Abx: Re-dose per guidelines (Cefoxitin: 1g <80kg; 2g ≥80kg)
  • Acetaminophen: 1000mg IV prior to emergence (if ≥6hrs after preop dose)
  • Transfuse: Hct <21% no CAD; <24% with CAD
  • Pelvic drain — JP and/or Foley at surgeon discretion
  • 6-Fr open-ended single-J ureteral stents
  • ½% Marcaine to all incision sites
🏥 Cystectomy: Postoperative ▼

Immediate Post-Op (PACU & Floor)

  • PACU Labs: CBCP, Basic
  • Abx: Cefoxitin (or alt) x 24hr then D/C
  • DVT: SCDs + Heparin 5000u SQ Q8H
  • Pain: Avoid narcotics; Acetaminophen 1000mg IV Q6H (PO when on clears); Toradol 15mg IV Q6H x 48hr (POD#1 if GFR>60)
  • GI: Famotidine 20mg IV Q12H day 0; transition PO when tolerating diet
  • Transfuse: 2U PRBC for HCT ≤21% (no CAD) or ≤24% (CAD); hold Toradol if any transfusion; hold heparin if >2U in 24hrs
  • Gum/hard candy Q2–4hr beginning POD 1

Daily Management (POD 1–4)

  • Neobladder: 60cc saline irrigations Q6H from POD 1; teach patient POD 2
  • Labs: CBCP, Basic, Ca, Mg, Phos POD 1–4; PRN thereafter
  • Ambulation: POD 0 OOB to chair; thereafter chair 6x/day, ambulate 6x/day; PT only if debilitated
  • Diet (Open): 8oz water Q8H POD 0; clears POD 1; regular POD 2
  • Diet (Robotic): Clears POD 0; regular POD 1
  • Lasix: 10mg IV POD 2 and 4 if renal function at baseline

Stent & Drain Management

  • Uncomplicated: Remove 1st stent if tolerating regular diet POD 3–4; 2nd stent 4hrs later or next AM. Cipro PO x1 1hr prior to 1st stent.
  • Complicated: Stents at surgeon discretion
  • Foley/Pelvic Drain (NOT neobladder catheter): Remove POD 1
  • JP: Send JP Cr prior to 1st stent, then 2hrs after 2nd stent; if serous, remove

Discharge Planning

  • Initiate D/C planning POD 1 or sooner
  • VNA 3x/wk weeks 1–2; 2x/wk weeks 3–4
  • Anticipate D/C POD 3–5; flatus and soft abdomen required
  • Neobladder: UCx from Foley on D/C day; 3-day culture-directed abx/antifungal starting day before Foley removal
  • Famotidine 20mg PO x 90 days
  • Apixaban 2.5mg BID x 30 days for DVT prophylaxis (all patients)
  • CBC & Basic 5–7 days post-D/C (can be local); consider hydration if dehydrated
  • Neobladder catheter: Remove 2 weeks post-op at nurse visit; cystogram prior. Reinforce self-cath/irrigations BID x 90 days.
Return Visits: 1–2 wk nurse (staple/stent PRN, CBC/Basic); 6 wk APP (renal US, CBC, Basic); 3 mo MD (CT A/P, Basic); then per surveillance.
📅 Post-Cystectomy Surveillance ▼

General Principles

  • Pathology discussion; letter to patient, PCP, referring urologist, and med onc
  • Refer high-risk (ypT2, pT3, variant histology, N+) to med onc at 6 weeks for adjuvant therapy
  • 6-week visit: APP + ET (if urostomy) with CMP, CBC, Renal US; consider Signatera
  • 3-month visit: Surgeon with imaging per risk categories and BMP
  • Further surveillance with APP per risk categories. Refer to home urologist when possible. Defer imaging to med onc if on adjuvant.

Low Risk (LG pTa)

  • 12mo and annually to year 5: Basic, Renal US with APP
  • Annual Basic with PCP to year 10

  • Yr 2–5 annually: Basic, Renal US, urine cytology with APP
  • Yr 5–10 annually: Basic with PCP/local urologist

High Risk (≥pT2, CIS, N+, M+, Positive Margin, Variant Histology, pT0 post-NAC)

  • 3mo: Basic, CTU with surgeon
  • Mo 6 & Q6mo to 2yr: Basic, Chest CT/CTU, urine cytology with APP
  • Yr 2–10 annually: Basic, Renal US, urine cytology with APP
Urethral Wash: At surgeon discretion; consider strongly for CIS or prostatic/urethral disease history.

🧬 Germline Genetic Testing ▼

Indications for Germline Testing

Offer germline testing to prostate cancer patients who have:

  • Positive family history of prostate cancer or related cancers
  • Personal history of breast cancer
  • Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry
  • GG 4/5 disease
  • Metastases (regional or distant)
  • GG 2/3 with cribriform/intraductal pathology
  • Personal history of: pancreatic, breast, colorectal, gastric cancer, melanoma, UTUC, glioblastoma, biliary tract, or small intestinal cancer

Pre-Test Counseling & Consent

Required: Spend a few minutes on pre-test education with patients and obtain signed Michigan Medicine consent (state law in Michigan). The consent PDF is embedded in the order set, and printed copies are also available in the Cancer Center clinic.

Financial Assistance

Encourage all patients to apply for financial assistance, even if they expect insurance coverage. This can reduce copays and deductibles. Out-of-pocket costs are generally capped at around $300, and in most cases there is no OOP cost.

Ordering & Specimen Collection

  • Blood draws can be done at U of M labs or via mobile phlebotomy. Choose one of these options when placing the order (search "Tempus xG" in MiChart).
  • Saliva is also an option if the patient lives far away and mobile phlebotomy is not feasible — a kit will be mailed to them.
Tip: Search "Tempus xG" in MiChart to find this order. Select Blood → Nurse or Phlebotomist (U of M labs) or Mobile Phlebotomist (Tempus) for collection.
🎯 Transperineal Prostate Biopsy ▼

1. Purpose

This document establishes division-wide standards for transperineal (TP) prostate biopsy — including indications, MRI requirements, technique selection, anesthesia planning, and repeat biopsy thresholds. The Division performs transperineal biopsy exclusively. The primary goal is to eliminate unwarranted clinical variation, particularly in biopsy indication decisions, MRI utilization, and fusion-targeting practices.

🎯 Primary Variability Targets
  • When to biopsy: applying consistent PSA, age-stratified, and kinetic thresholds across all providers.
  • MRI utilization: mpMRI is required before all biopsies — this is a division mandate, not a clinical preference.
  • Technique selection: standardizing when to use systematic-only vs. MRI-fusion combined targeting.

2. Indications for Biopsy

Biopsy decisions must integrate PSA level, PSA kinetics, PSA density, DRE findings, patient age, life expectancy, and MRI findings. No single threshold is absolute — shared decision-making with the patient is required and must be documented in the encounter note.

2a. PSA Thresholds — Initial Biopsy (Biopsy-Naïve Patients)

The following age-stratified thresholds are the division standard. Clinical context may justify biopsy below threshold or deferral above threshold, but rationale must be documented.

2c. Non-PSA Indications

The following scenarios indicate biopsy regardless of PSA level, provided life expectancy and patient goals support intervention:

  • Abnormal DRE: palpable nodule, induration, or asymmetry in any PSA range
  • PI-RADS 4–5 lesion on mpMRI with PSA ≥1.0 ng/mL
  • PI-RADS 3 lesion with PSA density ≥0.15 or PSAV >0.5 ng/mL/year
  • Elevated biomarker: PHI >35, 4Kscore >7.5%, or SelectMDx high-risk result with elevated or rising PSA
  • Prior ASAP or extensive high-grade PIN on any previous biopsy
  • Referred patient requiring tissue confirmation for clinical trial enrollment

2d. When NOT to Biopsy

Biopsy is generally not recommended in the following scenarios. When an exception is made, shared decision-making documentation is required.

Significant comorbidity (ECOG ≥3) where treatment would not be offered regardless of resultDo not biopsy; document rationale; palliative or supportive care referral if symptomatic
PSA elevation clearly attributable to acute prostatitis, instrumentation, or recent ejaculationTreat the cause; recheck PSA in 6–8 weeks before revisiting biopsy decision

📝 Shared Decision-Making Documentation Standard
  • For every biopsy decision — to proceed or defer — the encounter note must include: (1) PSA value and date, (2) DRE result, (3) MRI result or documented reason MRI was not obtained, (4) age and estimated life expectancy, (5) patient-stated goals, and (6) clinical rationale for the decision.
  • For patients age ≥70 or with significant comorbidity: a brief life expectancy assessment (Charlson index or clinical estimate) must be documented prior to proceeding.
  • Use of risk calculators (PCPT, ERSPC, 4Kscore, PHI) is encouraged and should be referenced in the note when used.

3. Pre-Biopsy MRI: Division Requirement

3a. MRI Requirements by Clinical Context

⚠️ mpMRI Is Required Before ALL Transperineal Prostate Biopsies
  • Multiparametric MRI (mpMRI) must be obtained and reviewed before any division provider performs a transperineal prostate biopsy. This is a non-negotiable division standard.
  • Proceeding to biopsy without mpMRI requires written documentation of the specific clinical exception and is subject to mandatory QI review at the next quarterly meeting.
  • MRI is required for initial biopsies, repeat biopsies, and Active Surveillance confirmatory biopsies. There is no scenario in which mpMRI is optional when biopsy is proceeding.

📋 MRI Exception Documentation — Required When MRI Is Not Obtained
  • The procedure note must explicitly document: (1) the specific reason MRI was not obtained, (2) how technique selection was made without MRI guidance, and (3) that the case is flagged for QI review.
  • Acceptable exceptions: urgent clinical scenario; patient with MRI contraindication (non-compatible implant); patient refusal after counseling.
  • Not acceptable: MRI not ordered, scheduling delays, or provider preference for systematic-only approach.

4. Biopsy Technique: Systematic vs. Targeted vs. Combined

The single most common source of variability in division practice is inconsistent application of MRI-fusion targeting. Technique selection must be based on MRI findings and documented prior to the procedure.

4a. Technique Selection by MRI Finding

📌 Division Standard: Combined Approach Is the Default When MRI Identifies a Lesion
  • When mpMRI identifies a PI-RADS ≥4 lesion, the required approach is combined: 12-core systematic + MRI-fusion targeted. Deviation requires explicit note documentation.
  • Systematic-only is appropriate only when MRI is PI-RADS 1–2, or PI-RADS 3 with a lesion <1cm and no high-risk features.
  • Cognitive targeting alone is not an acceptable substitute when software fusion hardware is available.
MRI FindingRequired TechniqueMin. Systematic CoresMin. Targeted Cores
PI-RADS 1–2 (negative)Systematic only12 coresNone
PI-RADS 3 — lesion <1 cmSystematic + clinical judgment12 cores0–2 at provider discretion (document rationale)
PI-RADS 3 — lesion ≥1 cmCombined (systematic + fusion targeted)12 cores2–4 via software fusion
PI-RADS 4Combined — required12 cores≥4 per lesion via software fusion
PI-RADS 5Combined — required12 cores≥6 per lesion via software fusion
PI-RADS 4–5; prior negative biopsyCombined + saturation template24–32 cores≥6 per lesion; tumor board discussion required prior
Multiple PI-RADS ≥4 lesionsCombined — each lesion targeted independently12 cores≥4 per lesion

4b. When Systematic-Only Is Acceptable — Documentation Required

If proceeding with systematic-only biopsy, the procedure note must confirm one of the following:

  • mpMRI result was PI-RADS 1–2 — reference report date and PI-RADS score in procedure note
  • mpMRI showed PI-RADS 3 with lesion <1cm and no high-risk features (PSA density <0.15, no DRE abnormality)
  • Fusion hardware unavailable on procedure date — document and schedule MRI-fusion re-biopsy within 60 days if PI-RADS ≤4

4c. Active Surveillance Biopsy

  • Use identical 12-zone nomenclature at every AS biopsy for direct cross-time comparison of prior positive sites
  • Re-target every previously positive site plus any new MRI lesion at every confirmatory biopsy
  • MRI lesion increase ≥2mm or PI-RADS upgrade: apply PI-RADS 4–5 protocol regardless of prior classification
  • Grade Group upgrade on AS biopsy: document in tumor board record; initiate treatment discussion within 3 weeks
✓ Core Labeling & Pathology Submission Standard
  • All cores labeled by 12-zone nomenclature: base/mid/apex × right/left × lateral/medial.
  • Targeted cores labeled by lesion designation (e.g., "Left mid anterior PI-RADS 4 lesion, core 1 of 4").
  • Fusion biopsy coordinates and software lesion ID referenced in procedure note.
  • Each core submitted in a separate, individually labeled container. No pooling.
  • Deviation from minimum core numbers requires a one-sentence justification in the procedure note.

📋 RCC IVC Thrombectomy: Centralization Policy ▼
Policy URO-ONC-001 | Effective: May 1, 2026 | Designated Sites: UPMC Presbyterian Hospital; UPMC Montefiore Hospital

This policy applies to all faculty within the UPMC Division of Urologic Oncology, advanced practice providers, residents, and fellows involved in the care of patients with RCC and IVC thrombus, operative scheduling coordinators and administrative personnel, and referring physicians both within and outside the UPMC health system.

1. Purpose

The Division of Urologic Oncology at UPMC has established this policy to ensure the highest standards of surgical care, interdisciplinary collaboration, and patient safety for patients with renal cell carcinoma (RCC) presenting with Level 2 inferior vena cava (IVC) tumor thrombus. Given the complexity, operative risk, and need for specialized vascular and hepatic surgical expertise inherent in these cases, all such procedures are centralized at a single designated site with defined multidisciplinary infrastructure.

2. Scope and Applicability

This policy applies to:

  • All faculty within the UPMC Division of Urologic Oncology
  • Advanced practice providers, residents, and fellows involved in the care of patients with RCC and IVC thrombus
  • Operative scheduling coordinators and administrative personnel responsible for surgical case booking
  • Referring physicians and advanced practice providers both within and outside the UPMC health system

3. Clinical Background

Renal cell carcinoma with venous tumor thrombus involves extension of tumor into the renal vein and, in a subset of patients, cephalad extension into the IVC. The Mayo Clinic classification system stratifies IVC thrombus by level of cephalad extension:

LevelClassificationAnatomic DefinitionComplexity
0Renal VeinThrombus confined to renal veinLow
IInfradiaphragmaticThrombus extends <2 cm above renal vein ostiumModerate
II ▶Infradiaphragmatic (High)Thrombus extends ≥2 cm above renal vein ostium but remains below diaphragm — POLICY APPLIESHigh — Designated Sites Only
III ▶SupradiaphragmaticThrombus extends above diaphragm, below right atrium — POLICY APPLIESVery High — Designated Sites Only
IV ▶IntracardiacThrombus extends into right atrium — POLICY APPLIESExtreme — Designated Sites Only

Level 2 thrombus requires infradiaphragmatic IVC control, mobilization of the liver, and potentially temporary hepatic vascular exclusion. Level 3 thrombus extends above the diaphragm and requires supradiaphragmatic IVC control, often necessitating thoracoabdominal exposure and cardiac anesthesia involvement. Level 4 thrombus extends into the right atrium and requires cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) for safe extraction. All three levels require operative expertise extending well beyond standard urologic oncology training and mandate the presence of a liver transplant surgeon with hepatic vascular control proficiency and confirmed cardiovascular surgery and CPB capability at the operating site.

4. Policy Statement

4.1 Designated Surgical Site

All surgical cases involving Level 2, Level 3, or Level 4 RCC IVC thrombectomy shall be performed exclusively at UPMC Presbyterian Hospital or UPMC Montefiore Hospital.

Both institutions have been vetted and confirmed to maintain full cardiovascular surgery services and on-site cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) capability — infrastructure that is required for Level 3 and Level 4 cases and must be available on standby for all Level 2 cases given the potential for intraoperative upstaging. Designation is based on the availability of:

  • Liver transplant surgical expertise with dedicated block time allocated to the Division of Urologic Oncology
  • Full cardiovascular surgery services with on-site cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) capability — vetted and confirmed at both designated sites
  • Cardiac surgery and perfusionist teams available for emergent CPB conversion if intraoperative hemodynamic instability or unanticipated intracardiac thrombus extension is identified
  • Institutional infrastructure for complex retroperitoneal and vascular surgery
  • High-acuity postoperative care including surgical ICU and hepatobiliary nursing expertise
  • Established multidisciplinary team relationships across Urologic Oncology, Hepatobiliary Surgery, Interventional Radiology, and Anesthesiology
  • Intraoperative transesophageal echocardiography (TEE) capability for thrombus level assessment

4.2 Mandatory Liver Transplant Surgeon Co-Surgeon Requirement

A UPMC liver transplant surgeon must be present as an operating surgeon — not a consultant — for all Level 2, 3, and 4 IVC thrombectomy cases. For Level 3 and 4 cases, cardiovascular surgery must additionally be engaged as an operating team. Cases may not be scheduled or performed without confirmed co-surgeon availability and allocated block time.

Roles and responsibilities of the liver transplant co-surgeon include:

  • Intraoperative hepatic mobilization and liver retraction as required for IVC exposure
  • Performance of hepatic vascular exclusion maneuvers (Pringle maneuver, hepatic vein clamping) when required for safe caval control
  • Management of intraoperative vascular complications involving the IVC, hepatic veins, or portal vasculature
  • Preoperative surgical planning participation including review of cross-sectional imaging

For Level 3 and Level 4 cases, additional cardiovascular surgery requirements apply:

  • Cardiovascular surgery attending must be present in the operating room for Level 3 cases requiring supradiaphragmatic IVC control
  • Cardiac surgery team with perfusionist and CPB circuit must be on standby and immediately available for all Level 3 cases; active CPB required for Level 4
  • Cardiac anesthesia involvement required for Level 3 and Level 4 cases; transesophageal echocardiography (TEE) mandatory
  • Thoracoabdominal or median sternotomy approach planning must be documented preoperatively for Level 3 and 4 cases

4.3 Liver Transplant Surgeon Block Time Allocation

The Division of Urologic Oncology maintains dedicated block time at both designated hospitals in coordination with the Division of Transplantation Surgery — and for Level 3 and 4 cases, the Division of Cardiothoracic Surgery — for the scheduling of Level 2 or higher IVC thrombectomy cases. The following scheduling requirements apply:

  • All Level 2, 3, and 4 RCC IVC thrombectomy cases must be booked within designated Urologic Oncology / Liver Transplant joint block time
  • Level 3 and 4 cases additionally require confirmed cardiovascular surgery team availability and CPB circuit reservation prior to booking
  • Scheduling coordinators must confirm co-surgeon availability prior to case booking
  • Cases requiring urgent or expedited scheduling must be coordinated directly between the Division Chief of Urologic Oncology and the liver transplant surgical team
  • Cases may not be placed in standard Urologic Oncology-only block time; non-compliance will trigger administrative review

5. Patient Eligibility and Case Identification

This policy applies to all patients meeting the following criteria:

  • Confirmed or highly suspected renal cell carcinoma (any histologic subtype) based on imaging or pathology
  • Radiographic evidence of IVC thrombus classified as Level 2, Level 3, or Level 4 (infradiaphragmatic extending ≥2 cm above the renal vein ostium, supradiaphragmatic, or intracardiac) as assessed by dedicated abdominal/pelvic MRI or CT venography with dedicated cardiac imaging for suspected Level 3–4
  • Surgical candidacy confirmed by multidisciplinary review

The responsible urologic oncology attending is accountable for:

  • Accurate preoperative thrombus level classification using dedicated cross-sectional imaging
  • Timely initiation of the case scheduling process in accordance with this policy
  • Referral to UPMC Presbyterian if the patient's care is being managed at another UPMC network site

6. Referral and Transfer of Care Requirements

Patients with Level 2, 3, or 4 RCC IVC thrombus identified at any UPMC network site must be referred to the Division of Urologic Oncology at UPMC Presbyterian Hospital or UPMC Montefiore Hospital for surgical planning and case execution. Accepting surgeons must have cross-coverage agreement and shared block time with a designated liver transplant co-surgeon, and the designated site must confirm cardiovascular surgery and CPB availability for the scheduled case date.

External referrals from non-UPMC institutions are welcomed and should be directed to the UPMC Urologic Oncology intake coordination line. Expedited consultation and imaging review will be facilitated upon referral.

7. Multidisciplinary Pre-operative Review

Prior to scheduling, the following multidisciplinary review steps are required:

  • Dedicated cross-sectional imaging review (MRI abdomen/pelvis with gadolinium preferred) to confirm thrombus level; cardiac MRI or CT chest for suspected Level 3–4 to define supradiaphragmatic or intracardiac extension
  • Cardiovascular surgery team notification and confirmation of CPB standby availability for the scheduled case date; active CPB team booking required for all Level 3 and Level 4 cases
  • Cardiac anesthesia consultation required for Level 3 and Level 4 cases; TEE planning documented
  • Anesthesiology pre-operative evaluation for high-risk vascular/cardiac surgical case
  • Cardiology or cardiac anesthesia consultation if TEE is planned or if intracardiac thrombus extension cannot be excluded
  • Interventional radiology involvement for consideration of preoperative IVC filter placement when clinically indicated
  • Preoperative oncology consultation for patients with metastatic disease in whom systemic therapy sequencing is relevant

8. Exceptions and Emergency Circumstances

Exceptions to this policy may occur in the following limited circumstances:
  • Life-threatening hemorrhage or acute hemodynamic instability requiring emergent operative intervention at a non-designated UPMC facility
  • Intraoperative reclassification of thrombus level that was not identifiable on preoperative imaging
In all exception cases, the Division Chief must be notified within 24 hours. A post-case review will be conducted, and cases will be logged for quality improvement tracking. Exceptions do not modify the prospective scheduling requirements of this policy.

9. Quality Improvement and Compliance Monitoring

The Division of Urologic Oncology will monitor compliance with this policy through the following mechanisms:

  • Quarterly case log review of all nephrectomy with IVC thrombectomy cases performed across UPMC network sites
  • Audit of operative records to confirm co-surgeon participation and site compliance
  • Review of any cases identified as non-compliant, with root cause analysis and corrective action plan as appropriate
  • Annual policy review with input from Urologic Oncology and Transplantation Surgery divisional leadership
